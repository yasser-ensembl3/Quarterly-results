# LVMH Moët Hennessy Louis Vuitton - Strategic Analysis
**Quarter:** Q3 2025

## Executive Summary

**TL;DR:** LVMH showed resilience with improving Q3 trends across all business groups despite challenging geopolitical and economic headwinds.

**Management Tone:** Cautiously Optimistic

The company maintained its powerful innovative momentum and demonstrated good resilience in a disrupted environment. Management remains confident and focused on enhancing brand desirability through authenticity, quality, and retail excellence while navigating economic uncertainty.

## Key Takeaways

1. LVMH demonstrated resilience with improving Q3 trends despite macro headwinds, showing the strength of its diversified luxury portfolio
2. Major creative leadership transitions across fashion houses are being well-received, potentially driving future growth through renewed brand energy
3. The shift from tourist to local demand is being successfully managed, with Europe and US showing solid local customer performance
4. Trade tensions continue to weigh on the important cognac business, particularly in US and China markets, representing a key risk to monitor
5. Sephora's market share gains and successful brand launches like Rhode demonstrate the strength of the selective retailing strategy

## Strategic Initiatives

### Global brand desirability enhancement strategy
- **Status:** in_progress
- **Progress:** Continuous focus on authenticity and quality of products, excellence in retail and agile organization
- **Expected Impact:** Reinforce global leadership position in luxury goods

### Creative leadership transitions across fashion houses
- **Status:** in_progress
- **Progress:** New creative directors appointed at multiple brands including Michael Rider at Celine, Jack McCollough and Lazaro Hernandez at Loewe, Sarah Burton at Givenchy, and Jonathan Anderson at Christian Dior
- **Expected Impact:** Contemporary refresh and renewed creative energy

### Tiffany & Co. store concept global rollout
- **Status:** in_progress
- **Progress:** Continued enhancement of iconic lines and global rollout of store concept inspired by The Landmark in New York, with recent openings in Milan and Tokyo showing high traffic and revenue
- **Expected Impact:** Enhanced brand positioning and retail experience

## Product & Innovation

### New Launches
- **La Beauté Louis Vuitton:** New creative makeup universe led by Dame Pat McGrath with focus on composition, innovation and sustainability
- **Miss Dior Essence and Dior Homme Parfum:** New fragrance launches from Parfums Christian Dior
- **TAG Heuer Carbonspring oscillator:** Revolutionary watch technology innovation
- **Bird on a Rock brooch by Tiffany & Co.:** New jewelry piece paying tribute to Jean Schlumberger's famous brooch design

### R&D Focus
Innovation in luxury materials, sustainable beauty formulations, and revolutionary watch technology including oscillator mechanisms
